Making sense of civic data

Independent designers and software engineers doing what they do best—making sense of complex problems by analyzing data and producing visualizations that speak for themselves.

Projects

We're working in the areas of education (SchoolCuts.org), criminal justice reform, political transparency, and Chicago community data. If you're active in any of these fields, let's talk.

You can read about our proposals to the Knight Foundation: Bringing school parents together around data, Crowdsourcing abandoned properties data and helping residents and cities act on that information, and Take the "Monkey Butt" out of city organizations.

Paul, CEO of Webitects and one of the founders of Open City, has been active in the open civic data movement since 2007. He spearheaded the creation of ChicagoLobbyists and worked on the designs of the CrimeInChicago and California Sentencing Institute websites. Learn more about specific data visualization approaches in a presentation that he and Nick gave to the 2012 Chicago WebConf. Currently, he is working on SchoolCuts.org.
Bilyan likes to take a design-thinking approach to vaguely-defined problems. He believes that there is no silver bullet for solving every problem—that success requires empathetic understanding of specific situations. This is key to applying scientific analysis of data in service of human needs. His work as President of Webitects—with clients such as the Obama 2008 campaign, Wikipedia, CREDO Mobile, Burns Institute, and Broadband Illinois—reflects this philosophy.
Forest is a mathematical sociology PhD student at the University of Chicago who plays pivotal roles in many open data projects. These include: a software library that uses machine learning to determine which records in a database refer to the same thing; an app that helps school parents applying for selective enrollment schools find the economic tier indicator affecting their application options; and Clear Streets, a map of recently plowed Chicago streets, using data from the City's live snow Plow Tracker. Find more projects at Forest's Github page. In a previous life he wrote SVO: Powering Your Vehicle With Straight Vegetable Oil
Nick's a designer who's passionate about finding new ways to explore data but knows just enough from his programming days to be dangerous. He is a founder of Open City and hands-on with many open data projects, including CrimeInChicago, California Sentencing Institute, Cook County budget site Look at Cook, SchoolCuts.org, and an innovative look at Metra's 2012 proposed service changes.
Elnaz's community organizing experience as former Student Government President at Illinois Institute of Technology (graduated 2012) comes in handy in her work at the Open Data Institute. She is one of the leaders of the SchoolCuts project, which ODI is working on with Apples2Apples. She has also been interviewing students, parents, teachers, and administrators as part of the research to support a related Chicago schools project. Besides design research and website concept development, she also writes code. Learn more about Elnaz.
Anna Marie is a web developer whose previous life in publications and design continue to influence her work. Between her undergraduate studies in Communication Arts at the University of the Philippines and her graduate work in Computer Science at Cleveland State University, her interests are broad. She often finds herself at the intersection of those divergent fields and is most creative when she collaborates with others on projects that combine both. Recent efforts include SchoolCuts.org and SaveObando.org.

Patrick is a chronicler of urban issues and a dabbler in software and data. He started 30 years ago as a journalist for Chicago and national publications, then shifted to nonprofits, where his work as a "scribe" gives him an insider's view of the needs of neighborhoods. Though he can't code in any language, he knows what he wants from data sources, and is contributing to design and usability for ODI's Chicago Fact Book Online project.

About

The Open Data Institute employs open civic data to provide fresh insights into pressing issues. We develop data visualizations, create data tools, and conduct workshops and presentations. Our audiences include citizens, community leaders, students, educators, and researchers. We are a nonprofit incorporated in the State of Illinois.
